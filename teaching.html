Teaching & Supervision

Dr. Godwin Edet Essoh has extensive teaching experience at undergraduate and postgraduate levels, with a strong commitment to academic excellence, mentorship, and applied learning.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach emphasizes critical thinking, practical application of theory, and student engagement. I encourage analytical reasoning, ethical reflection, and real-world problem solving, particularly within public administration, governance, and human resource management.

Courses Taught

  • Public Administration
  • Public Policy Analysis
  • Employment Relations
  • Human Resource Management
  • Industrial Relations
  • Peace and Conflict Studies
  • Development Studies
  • Management Principles

Curriculum & Programme Development

Dr. Essoh has contributed significantly to curriculum design and programme development, including the design of postgraduate and undergraduate programmes in Peace and Development Studies and Public Administration. Several of these programmes have achieved full National Universities Commission (NUC) accreditation.

Student Supervision

He has supervised numerous undergraduate projects and postgraduate theses across Public Administration, Industrial Relations, Business Administration, and Development Studies.

  • Undergraduate research projects (Ritman University, Salem University, NOUN)
  • Master’s degree theses in Public Administration and Business Administration
  • Academic mentorship for final-year students and early-career researchers
